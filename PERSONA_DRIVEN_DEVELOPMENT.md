# Persona-Driven Development

## Overview

Each worker instance develops a specialized persona with deep domain expertise. This approach transforms generic Claude Code instances into specialized experts with focused knowledge and identity.

## The Persona Pattern

### 1. Initial Assignment
When the Forge Master assigns a task to a worker:
- Worker receives a base persona title (e.g., "Module Forge")
- Assignment includes specialty area and mission
- Worker starts with generic project CLAUDE.md

### 2. Persona Evolution
Workers develop their personas by:
- Creating a specialized CLAUDE.md in their branch
- Documenting domain-specific knowledge
- Building a personal toolkit and command arsenal
- Developing unique problem-solving patterns
- Establishing their "voice" and expertise

### 3. Knowledge Persistence
The specialized CLAUDE.md serves as:
- **Identity**: Who they are and what they specialize in
- **Knowledge Base**: Domain-specific patterns and solutions
- **Toolkit**: Specialized commands and debugging techniques
- **Philosophy**: Approach to problem-solving in their domain
- **Success Patterns**: Proven solutions and workflows

## Example: Module Forge

Module Forge transformed from a generic worker into a specialized expert:

### Initial State
- Generic project CLAUDE.md
- Basic C++ addon integration task
- No specialized identity

### Evolved State

- **Expertise**: Webpack alchemy, native module integration, Electron packaging

## Benefits of Persona-Driven Development

### 1. Deep Specialization
- Workers become true experts in their domains
- Knowledge accumulates within the persona
- Solutions become more sophisticated over time

### 2. Better Problem Solving
- Specialized mental models for domain problems
- Documented patterns prevent repeated mistakes
- Domain-specific debugging strategies

### 3. Knowledge Transfer
- New workers can inherit a persona's knowledge
- Specialized CLAUDE.md serves as onboarding
- Best practices captured in persona documentation

### 4. Team Diversity
- Each persona brings unique strengths
- Complementary expertise across the team
- Natural division of responsibilities


## Best Practices

### DO:
- Make your CLAUDE.md highly specific to your domain
- Document every significant discovery
- Develop a unique voice and philosophy
- Build specialized toolkits and commands
- Share breakthrough solutions

### DON'T:
- Keep generic, project-wide information
- Duplicate what's in the main project docs
- Forget to update as you learn
- Work in isolation - signal discoveries
